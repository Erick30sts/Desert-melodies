Egyptian music has a history of over 5000 years. It turns out that, in ancient Egypt, music was quite related to religion, ceremonies, and daily life; Egyptians used music for both celebrations and festivities, as well as for funerary rites. In ancient Egypt, music was used as a way to worship the gods; various and different instruments were used, such as the lyre, the harp, and the sistrum; moreover, representations of musicians were found in the tombs of the pharaohs, highlighting their cultural importance. The arrival of Islam also had an impact on the musical aspect, resulting in blends and influences from Arab and Persian music. In the 19th and 20th centuries, Egyptian music continued to show significant advancements, with the emergence of figures like Umm Kulthum, who became a cultural icon in the music world; today, Egypt continues to be a major influence in Arab music, as well as its presence in the pop world and the film industry.
