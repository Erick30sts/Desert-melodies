Classical Arabic Music

Characterized by the complexity of melodies, often accompanied by improvisations that result in pieces of great beauty and cultural richness. Additionally, it makes use of scales called maqamat, which are essentially Arabic musical modes with a distinctive sound.

Folk Music

This term refers to Egypt's traditional songs, which vary depending on the region but are generally performed during festivals and special celebrations.

Sufi Music

Refers to mystical practices in Sufism. Egyptian Sufi music seeks spiritual ecstasy and is performed in religious contexts, such as the "whirling dervishes" ceremonies.

Modern and Pop Music

In the 20th century, many artists became icons of modern Egyptian music, such as Umm Kulthum, Abdel Halim Hafez, and Mohamed Abdel Wahab, successfully blending traditional music with some Western influences. Today, artists like Amr Diab and Tamer Hosny have gradually introduced Egyptian music to international audiences.

Cultural Influences and Modernization

Over the years, Egypt has experienced a fusion of traditional music with Western influences. Since the early 20th century, jazz bands, rock, and pop from around the world have influenced Egyptian music. However, many contemporary artists have sought to preserve traditional roots in their musical compositions.
